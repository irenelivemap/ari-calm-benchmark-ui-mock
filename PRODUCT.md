# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Route researchers, product designers, and routing engineers testing whether a calm walking route is perceived as calmer than a faster alternative. Testers may be internal or external and should understand the task without needing team context.

## Product Purpose

This mock provides a map-first blinded A/B testing UI for ARI calm walking routes. Success means a tester can start a session, inspect both routes clearly, choose the route they would actually take for the calm situation, and submit structured answers that a routing engineer can connect to real calm/fast route data later.

## Brand Personality

Clear, calm, focused. The interface should feel friendly enough for external testers, but precise enough for research and model evaluation.

## Anti-references

Avoid cluttered survey pages, generic dashboards, marketing-style landing pages, hidden map interactions, tiny map previews, repeated explanatory copy, and anything that makes the route comparison feel secondary to the form.

## Design Principles

- Map first: route visibility, zooming, fit-to-route, and alternate map view are core to the task.
- Blind but understandable: Route A and Route B stay unlabeled, while the tester always knows what decision they are making.
- Progressive detail: essential instructions stay visible; definitions and research context remain accessible without clutter.
- One source of truth: avoid repeating duration, route purpose, or data-use explanations across the page.
- Ingestible by engineering: the UI should stay separable from the model/backend through a clear route-pair and answer contract.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for readable contrast, keyboard-operable controls, visible focus states, reduced-motion support for decorative animation, and copy that works for testers outside the ARI team.
